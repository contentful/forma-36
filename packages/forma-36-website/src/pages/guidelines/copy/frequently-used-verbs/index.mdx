# Frequently used verbs

This section contains frequently used verbs within our application, many of which can be used in different ways depending on the context. To ensure clear navigation for our users, consider the context when using instructional verbs. All definitions are based on the Merriam-Webster dictionary.

| Term        | Type | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                  | Example                                 |
| ----------- | ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| **Build**   | Verb | To develop according to a systemic plan or definite process. <br/>(Consider what actions you are asking the user to do to determine if this is the right word to use. It can often be replaced with Create). | “Build the database”                    |
| **Create**  | Verb | To make or bring something new into existence.                                                                                                                                                               | “Create a content type”                 |
| **Enter**   | Verb | To put in / insert.                                                                                                                                                                                          | “Enter your name”                       |
| **Filter**  | Verb | Remove options. <br/>(Must be used in the context of other options)                                                                                                                                          | “Filter the search results”             |
| **Save**    | Verb | To look into or over carefully or thoroughly in an effort to find or discover something. <br/>(Consider when to use search vs. filter).                                                                      | “Search for a content type”             |
| **Save**    | Noun | An act of searching                                                                                                                                                                                          | “Filter your search”                    |
| **Select**  | Verb | To choose (as by fitness or excellence) from a number or group. <br/>(Use in the context of other options).                                                                                                  | “Select your state” (From dropdown)     |
| **Set**     | Verb | To apply or define, may be relevant if the user is defining something for the first time.                                                                                                                    | “Set your preferences”                  |
| **Set up**  | Verb | To make carefully worked out plans in preparation. To put (a machine) in readiness or adjustment for an operation.                                                                                           | “Set up your terminal”                  |
| **Setup**   | Noun | Preparation and adjustment for an assigned task. <br/>(Often used with “the“ or “a“ before).                                                                                                                 | “Follow setup instructions“             |
| **Specify** | Verb | To name or state explicitly or in detail. <br/>(Can often be replaced with “Set”).                                                                                                                           | “Specify the color and quantity below.” |
| **Submit**  | Verb | To present or propose to another for review, consideration, or decision. <br/>(Consider context: This word has a formal tone and can be replaced with something lighter).                                    | “He submitted his resignation.”         |
