# Frequently used verbs

This section contains frequently used verbs within our application, many of which can be used in different ways depending on the context. To ensure clear navigation for our users, consider the context when using instructional verbs. All definitions are based on the Merriam-Webster dictionary.

## Build

Verb  
To develop according to a systemic plan or definite process. (Consider what actions you are asking the user to do to determine if this is the right word to use. It can often be replaced with Create).  
EX: “Build the database”

## Create

Verb  
To make or bring something new into existence.  
EX: “Create a content type”

## Enter

Verb  
To put in / insert  
EX: “Enter your name”

## Filter

Verb  
Remove options.
(Must be used in the context of other options).  
EX: “Filter the search results”

## Save

Verb  
To preserve or guard from injury, destruction, or loss  
EX: “Save this file before closing?”

## Search

Verb  
To look into or over carefully or thoroughly in an effort to find or discover something.  
(Consider when to use search vs. filter).  
EX: “Search for a content type” -
Noun  
An act of searching  
EX: “Filter your search”

## Select

Verb  
To choose (as by fitness or excellence) from a number or group.  
(Use in the context of other options).  
EX: “Select your state” (From dropdown)

## Set

Verb  
To apply or define, may be relevant if the user is defining something for the first time.  
EX: “Set your preferences”

## Set up

Verb  
To make carefully worked out plans in preparation. To put (a machine) in readiness or adjustment for an operation.  
EX: “Set up your terminal”

## Setup

Noun  
Preparation and adjustment for an assigned task.  
(Often used with “the“ or “a“ before).  
EX: “Follow setup instructions“

## Specify

Verb  
To name or state explicitly or in detail.  
(Can often be replaced with “Set”).  
EX: “Specify the color and quantity below.”

## Submit

Verb  
To present or propose to another for review, consideration, or decision  
(Consider context: This word has a formal tone and can be replaced with something lighter).  
EX: “He submitted his resignation.”
