# Suggested Reading
We’re grateful to teams that have shared their process and approach through their design systems and copy style guides. 
Here’s a few of our favorites:

- Google Material Design System (https://material.io/) 
- Mail Chimp Content Style Guide (https://styleguide.mailchimp.com)
- IBM Carbon Design System (http://carbondesignsystem.com/guidelines/content/general)
- Pegasystems Tone and Voice Style Guide (https://bolt-design-system.com/docs/principles/interface-copy.html)
- Salesforce Lightning Design System (https://www.lightningdesignsystem.com/assets/downloads/salesforce-voice-and-tone.pdf)

Another shoutout goes to talented writers and designers who shared their process through blog posts. These thoughtful articles on microcopy, testing UX copy, and specific interactions such as error messages got us excited and empowered as we created our own UX copy guide.

- Why microcopy is a lot more than UX writing ( https://blog.prototypr.io/why-microcopy-is-a-lot-more-than-ux-writing-cfa2a0281dc3)
- How to rock product copy on the fly (https://www.invisionapp.com/inside-design/how-to-rock-product-copy-on-the-fly)
- How to write a perfect error message (https://uxplanet.org/how-to-write-a-perfect-error-message-da1ca65a8f36)
- The microcopyist- cancellation, confirmation, conflagration (microcopy for destructive actions) (https://uxdesign.cc/the-microcopyist-cancellation-confirmation-conflagration-8a6047a4cf9)
- Microinteractions: small details matter (https://uxdesign.cc/microinteractions-detailed-design-9113c88946d0)
- The ROI of UX writing (https://blog.prototypr.io/the-roi-of-ux-writing-ae69b4029ec2)
