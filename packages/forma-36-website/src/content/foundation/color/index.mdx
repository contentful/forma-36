## Colors overview

### Semantic

<ColorSwatchGroup group={semanticColors} />

### Gray

Gray is our main color used for typography.

<ColorSwatchGroup group={grayColors} />

### Blue

Blue is our primary color and should be used when you want to highlight
a primary action.

<ColorSwatchGroup group={blueColors} />

### Green

Green is used to highlight positive actions or messages. E.g. when
showing a success message, a save action, or a marking a step as
complete.

<ColorSwatchGroup group={greenColors} />

### Red

Red is used to highlight negative actions or messages. E.g. when performing
a distructive action such as deleting content, for highlighting validation
or application error messages, or for showing statuses with a negative
context.

<ColorSwatchGroup group={redColors} />

### Purple
<ColorSwatchGroup group={purpleColors} />

### Orange

Orange is used to highlight actions or messages which require attention but
don't necessarily have a negative context. E.g. showing a draft/unpublished
state, or showing a gentle warning message rather than an error message.

<ColorSwatchGroup group={orangeColors} />

## Accessibility

Contentful palette contains 9 values from `100` to `900`. For most of the color groups, black text is WCAG AA accessible on colors ranging from `100` to `400`, white text is accessible on colors from `500` to `900`. The only outlier is the yellow group, where black is accessible on colors ranging from `100` to `700` and white on the `yellow-800` and `yellow-900`
